Chole bhature is a food dish popular in the Northern areas of the Indian subcontinent. It is a combination of chana masala and bhatura/puri, a deep-fried bread made from maida. Although it is known as a typical Punjabi dish, It is originally a culinary dish of Delhi and Uttar Pradesh.

Ingredients

For Bhature

  • 1½ cupRefined flour,
  • ½ tspSugar
  • Salt to taste
  • ½ tsp Oil
  • 5 gms Dry Yeast soaked in water & sugar,
  • Water
  • 2 tbsp Semolina, soaked in water
  • 1 tsp Oil

For Pressure Cooking Chole

  • 1 ½ cups Chickpeas, soaked, overnight
  • 4-5 Dry Amla
  • 1 Dry red chilli
  • 2 Black cardamom
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp Baking soda
  • 1 Bay leaf
  • 2 tbsp Tea powder
  • Water as required

Steps

Bhature

  1. In a parat, add refined flour, sugar, salt to taste, oil, dry yeast soaked in water, semolina soaked in water for 1 hour and mix well making sure everything is well combined.
  2. Further, add water as required and knead the dough.
  3. Knead to the smooth and soft dough without putting much pressure.
  4. Grease the dough with oil, cover and rest for 1 hours.
  5. Pinch a ball sized dough and make a ball without any cracks.
  6. Roll slightly thick, applying oil to prevent it from sticking.
  7. Cover and rest for 15 minutes.
  8. Finally, roll the dough evenly into circles using a rolling pin. roll neither too thin or thick.
  9. Heat oil in a deep kadai. When the oil is sufficiently hot, add one bhatura.
  10. Press with the spoon to puff up.
  11. Also once they begin to puff, splash oil over the bhatura till they puff completely.
  12. Flip over and fry the bhatura till golden brown all over.
  13. Then drain the bhature into tissue paper to remove excess oil. Fry all bhature the same way.

Chole

  1. In a pressure cooker, add soaked chickpeas, dry amla, dry red chilli, black cardamom, salt to taste, baking soda, bay leaf, tea powder and water as required.
  2. Cover and pressure cook for 5 whistles. If you do not have tea bags, then you can prepare tea decoction and add it to the cooker.
  3. Once the pressure releases, open the cooker and discard the tea bags. keep aside.
  4. Strain the chloe and keep the remaining water for later use.
